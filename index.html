Who We Are

FEMME-CVD is an international consortium dedicated to understanding the molecular and immune interactions driving cardiovascular disease in postmenopausal women.

By combining expertise from basic science, translational research and clinical practice, the consortium aims to generate high-impact knowledge that improves women’s cardiovascular health.

Cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death worldwide, and their impact on women has long been underestimated—particularly during and after menopause. The hormonal changes associated with menopause increase cardiovascular risk, affecting blood vessels, cholesterol levels, and metabolic health, yet women remain underdiagnosed and undertreated. Addressing this critical gap is the mission of our transatlantic consortium of highly renowned researchers, bringing together leading experts from across Europe and North America. By combining multidisciplinary expertise, cutting-edge science, and international collaboration, the consortium is dedicated to advancing knowledge, improving prevention strategies, and transforming cardiovascular care for women during the menopausal transition and beyond.

Consortium Members

Dr Inés Pineda-Torra

Principal Investigator (EU Network Lead)

Expert in cardiovascular and immunology.

Dr. Pineda-Torra is a biochemist with 30 years of international experience in transcriptional regulation of lipid metabolism by lipid-activated transcription factors, and the crosstalk between lipid metabolism and immune response in metabolic and autoimmune diseases. Her work has helped to define the molecular and metabolic pathways underlying enhanced cardiovascular risk in SLE and the metabolic and immune shifts upon sex hormone changes. Full Professor in Cardiometabolic Medicine at University College London she is now Director of the Department of Integrative Physiopathology at CABIMER, Spain, where she leads the Metabolism, Immunity and Cardiovascular risk group. She is the scientific coordinator of two major academic-industry consortia (HARMONi & AVANTE) aiming to develop novel female-focused cardiovascular risk algorithms through the integration of multi-omic analyses with clinical variables. She is the recipient of the prestigious Axa Chair Health 2023. Through these national and international collaborations, her group now focuses on sex- and disease-specific differences in immune cell lipid metabolism, elucidating molecular mechanisms of cardiovascular risk in women and how menopause alters disease trajectories through the immuno-modulatory actions of female sex hormones.

Dr Harmony Reynolds

Principal Investigator (USA Network Lead)

Cardiologist specializing in postmenopausal cardiovascular risk.

Dr Reynolds is a distinguished cardiologist at NYU Langone Health in New York and a leading international expert in women’s cardiovascular health. She serves as Director of the Sarah Ross Soter Center for Women’s Cardiovascular Research and holds the prestigious Saul J. Farber Professorship in Medicine, focusing her clinical and investigative work on heart disease syndromes that disproportionately affect women, including heart attacks with non-obstructive arteries and related conditions. Dr. Reynolds’s research—supported by major grants from institutions like the American Heart Association and the National Institutes of Health—aims to close the gap in diagnosis and treatment of heart disease in women by uncovering underlying causes and optimal care strategies. Her commitment to patient-centered care, education, and groundbreaking science has earned her recognition such as the 2024 Bernadine Healy Leadership in Women’s Cardiovascular Disease Award.

Dr Mercedes Ricote

Molecular Biology Lead

Researcher in molecular signaling pathways.

Dr Ricote is a molecular biologist whose research focuses on the role of nuclear receptors in the regulation of metabolism and immune function in both physiological and pathological contexts. Her work has contributed to defining how metabolic pathways control macrophage activation and influence cardiovascular homeostasis and disease. She is currently a group leader at the National Centre for Biotechnology (CNB-CSIC) in Madrid, Spain, where she leads research programs aimed at unraveling the mechanisms by which nuclear receptors coordinate metabolism, inflammation, and mother-to-neonate signaling in the control of energy homeostasis. She has led multiple national- and EU-funded collaborative research programs and actively contributes to national and international networks focused on metabolism, inflammation, and the transcriptional and epigenetic regulation of obesity and cardiovascular disease.

Dr Nicolas Venteclef

Expert in inmmunometabolism and Diabetes Disease

Specialist in bioinformatics and cardiovascular data analysis.

Dr Venteclef is an immunometabolism researcher whose work focuses on the molecular and cellular mechanisms linking immune function to metabolic homeostasis and diabetes. His research has helped define how innate immune cells, particularly macrophages, regulate insulin sensitivity and pancreatic β-cell function through inflammatory and epigenetic pathways, thereby influencing susceptibility to cardiometabolic disease. He is a group leader at the Necker Institute in Paris, France, where he leads the IMMEDIAB team (Immunity and Metabolism of Diabetes). His research program investigates how immune–metabolic crosstalk is shaped by environmental cues and epigenomic regulation in physiological and pathological contexts, with a strong emphasis on human diabetes and its cardio-metabolic-renal complications. Dr. Venteclef is the recipient of an ERC Consolidator Grant (Epifat) and leads the EIU-funded project Intercept-T2D, both supporting his work on immune and epigenetic determinants of diabetes risk and progression. Through these and other collaborative programs, he actively contributes to European and international research networks in immunometabolism, inflammation, and precision cardiometabolic medicine.

Dr Kathryn Moore

Expert in cardiovascular and immunology

Focuses on clinical trial design and patient recruitment.

Dr Moore, PhD is an internationally acclaimed cardiovascular scientist at NYU Langone Health, where she serves as the Jean and David Blechman Professor of Cardiology and Director of the Cardiovascular Research Center. Her pioneering research focuses on the molecular mechanisms driving atherosclerosis, chronic inflammation, and cholesterol metabolism, with the goal of uncovering innovative therapeutic targets to prevent and treat cardiovascular disease. Dr. Moore’s work has earned prestigious honors, including the Lefoulon-Delalande Institut de France Grand Prize in Science, election to the National Academy of Sciences, and recognition as a Distinguished Scientist by the American Heart Association, reflecting her profound impact on cardiovascular biology and translational medicine..

Dr Kendall Nettles

Immunology Lead

Expert in immune signaling and inflammation.

Dr Nettles is an accomplished biomedical researcher and Associate Professor in the Department of Cancer Biology at The Herbert Wertheim UF Scripps Institute for Biomedical Innovation & Technology in Florida, where he leads a structural biology laboratory dedicated to designing novel therapeutic strategies for hormone-sensitive cancers such as breast and prostate cancer. His research focuses on understanding how hormones and their receptors function at the molecular level to drive disease progression and treatment resistance, and on creating innovative compounds that improve patient outcomes while minimizing side effects. With a distinguished academic background, including a PhD from the University of Chicago and a long history of NIH-funded projects, Dr. Nettles is nationally recognized for his contributions to translational cancer biology and molecular therapeutics..
